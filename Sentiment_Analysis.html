Sentiment Analysis Project¶

Goal¶

The goal of this project is to analyze a sentiment dataset containing user posts across multiple platforms.
We aim to clean the data, explore trends, and gain insights into user behavior, sentiment distribution, and engagement patterns (likes, retweets, activity).

Tools Used¶

  • Python (Pandas, NumPy)
  • Visualization: Matplotlib, Seaborn
  • Jupyter Notebook

Insights from EDA¶

  • Sentiment Distribution: Most of the posts are Positive, followed by Neutral. Negative posts are the least common.
  • Posts per Platform: Instagram has the highest activity compared to other platforms.
  • Posts per Weekday: Engagement is higher during weekdays (especially Monday and Tuesday).
  • Retweets by Sentiment: Positive posts tend to receive slightly more retweets compared to negative or neutral ones.
  • Likes by Sentiment: Positive posts clearly get more likes on average.

Conclusion¶

  • Positive sentiment dominates the dataset, showing overall favorable discussions.
  • User activity is higher on specific platforms (like Twitter/Instagram) and peaks during certain hours.
  • Engagement metrics (likes, retweets) vary strongly by sentiment type.
  • Yearly sentiment trends show how discussions have evolved over time.

This analysis demonstrates how sentiment and engagement can be explored through data cleaning, visualization, and insights.
